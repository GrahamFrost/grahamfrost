Computer Building - Project | Reflection

by Graham

This was a part of my final project for NSCC. One of the tasks that was decided aupon was to add all of the components from the case to a case made entirely of extruded aluminium. This required to take parts of metal and drill holes into them so that the components could be mounted to the rail systems on the aluminium. I learned that custom built cases are interesting, however the open case design is a horrible long term plan as dust accumlates quickly and there is no buffer between your parts and any potential dangerous environmental hazards. Another downside to the open case design is that airflow is greatly reduced. For systems using the tradtional air cooled system for their CPU's, problems may arise with heat levels.
